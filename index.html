Sir David Attenborough

Naturalist and National Treasure


A Quick Timeline

1926
The legend is born in Isleworth, Middlesex, UK.
1936
A young David Attenborough attends a lecture by Archibald Belaney and is inspired by his knowledge and views on conservatism and ecological systems.
1945
Attenborough wins a scholorship to Clare College, Cambridge to read Geology and Zoology.
1947
He is conscripted to the Royal Navy as part of national service.
1950
Attenborough marries Jane Elizabeth Ebsworth Oriel and applies to work at the British Broadcasting Corporation.
1952
Having been rejected for the role of radio broadcaster, his CV is later picked up and, despite never owning one, he begins his career in television.
1973
After rising through the ranks at the BBC making his way up to being Director of Programmes with talks of him being the next Director-General of the BBC, Attenborough quits in order to focus on writing and presenting.
1979
Life on Earth is released to critical acclaim, presented by Attenborough himself. This is to be the first of many in the Life series.
Present
(2020)
Sir David has now been involved in a great number of nature documentaries and was involved in 4 of the Top 10 highest rated television programmes on IMDB, including numbers 1 and 2.
In addition to his shows being incredibly well received by the public and critics alike, he has been awarded many commendations and honours for his work including his knighthood and over 30 honorary degrees. At least 20 species and a polar research ship have also been named after him.

If anything on this page has inspired you to learn more about Sir David and his astonishing list of accomplishments, I reccommend giving his wikipedia page a read or better still watching some of his award winning programmes.
